What is Stoicism?


Stoicism is a school of Hellenistic philosophy founded by the greek philosopher Zeno in the 3rd century BCE. The four main virtues of stoicism are: Courage, Temperance, Justice, and Wisdom. The stoics believe that our wealth, status, power, possesion and stature are neither good or bad, and that they have no importance in our relationships with each other.

Famous Stoics:

Cato

95BC-April 46BC. Roman Senator during the late republic. Famously opposed Julius Ceasar to the point where he was the only man Ceasar could never forgive. Took his own life instead of surrendering to Ceasar and became a Martyr for the Roman Rebulic. General George Washington showed Revolutionary troops a play based on Cato to inspire them during the Winter at Valley Forge.

Give me a sword so that I may set my country free.

Marcus Aurelius

26 April 121CE-17 March 180CE. Roman emperor from 7 March 161-17 March 180. He was the last of the 5 good emperors of Rome. His personal diary "Meditations" became one of the most influential and prominent texts of the stoic philosophy. He wasn't born in line of succession to become emperor but was adopted by Antoninius to be his successor to the throne.

Be strict with yourself and tolerant of others.



Epictetus

50CE-135CE. Was born into slavery at Hierapolis, Phrygia and he lived in Rome until he was banished to Greece. His teachings were written down by one of his students. The word "Epictetus" in Greek means "Gained or Acquired" and his real name is unknown. Taught stoicism as a way of life and not just to be a philosophy.

No man is free who is not master of himself.

Seneca

4BCE-65CE. Wrote plays and was a Roman statesman in addition to being a stoic philosopher. He was banished from Rome for 8 years by emperor Claudius and was brought back to tutor emperor Nero. All of his plays were tragedies. Was forced to take his own life by emperor Nero who believed he was involved in a conspiracy against him.

I am not a wise man and I never will be.
